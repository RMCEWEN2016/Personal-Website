CAD Programs I Use

There are so many different CAD programs out there, so what programs do I prefer to use?

SolidWorks

By far my most preferred CAD program. Used quite often in Mechanical Design of all sorts, including Welding and Stress Simulation, not just 3D parametric modelling. I use SolidWorks nearly everyday at home for personal projects and rapid prototyping with my 3D printer. Check out my projects here. Click here to see my SolidWorks work.

Autodesk Revit

Revit is an architectural design CAD program that allows VERY in-depth design for the user. You can control anything from mapping the topography of the land your want to build on to the showing the position of the sun year-round at those co-ordinates to get a bang for your buck with your windows. Click here to see my Revit work.

Autodesk AutoCAD

AutoCAD is my lesser preferred CAD program. I really only use it for 2D design drawings, like ones you can make in SolidWorks at the click of a button. The difference with this is you can control each individual element, like the line colors, which is important if your industry has this as a standard. Click here to see my AutoCAD work.

Autodesk Maya

Maya is a box-modelling CAD program, often used in the animation industry. Pixar is probably the most well known company that uses this program. Since this is a serious tool, it comes with a high learning curve, of which I have definitely not conquered, but it is very enjoyable to mess around in it. Click here to see my Maya work.
