PNY Flashdrive

As a student that is taking a lot of computer courses and also works on the computer a lot at home, a flashdrive is my best friend. Being able to take a flashdrive and slap it onto my keys is a godsend if you like physical file transfer and not having to mess with clouds. I use Google Drive for primarily essays and slideshows, but never really any other files because they aren't already in a format that I can develop within Google Drive itself. Google Drive has Slides for slideshows and Docs for writing papers, but they don't have CAD programs or a built in coding program. It's a lot easier to me to save files in a format that I'm used to than messing around in Google Drive and their foreign file heirarchy design.
I carry around a USB 3.0 PNY Flashdrive with me wherever I go, but I eventually came across a problem. I almost lost it because it came off of my keys because of an engineering mistake. Instead of figuring out a way to somehow lock the 2 injection-molded peices together, they left them as is. This then weakens over use, and eventually people who use this product on a daily basis WILL lose it along with all their important documents within it. I've come up with a fix for it.
